    Braves Coding Challenge

    Griffin Ansel

    Hello and welcome to my solution to the coding challenge posed by the Atlanta Braves. This site is intended to show that I have a foundation in web development, familiarity with baseball data, and an ability to visualize data in an easily ingestible format. As my resume does not include much to attest to my skill in web development but has significant baseball experience, I focused primarily on designing the site itself rather than going in depth on analyzing the baseball data. On the Scouting Report page, you will see two different graph types to visualize the data given to me in PitchData.csv.

    After growing up a season ticket holder for the Oakland A's, I had the opportunity to work in the A's Baseball Operations Department within their front office for two summers. That work was a dream come true; I worked on the A's draft strategy and announced a pick at the 2018 MLB draft (RHP Chad Bryant II who, incidentally, was drafted by the Braves the next year), analyzed minor league and collegiate data to find trends valuable to our MLB club, and evaluated prospects trades at the deadline. Now that I'm in my final year at MIT, I am looking for a full time job I'd enjoy. I hope that job can come in Atlanta with the Braves!

    If you'd like to learn more about me, please click the About Me tab at the top of the page, my name at the bottom of the page, or here to be redirected to my personal website, which includes links to my LinkedIn, GitHub, and Resume!
